# English or French?

## Working language (current state)
- it is the language in which I think and write with the most precision,
- it also reflects my current professional context.

During this early phase, priority is given to:
- clarity of thought,
- structuring ideas,
- internal coherence of the project.

---

## Expected evolution

The transition to English is **inevitable**.

If this project is to:
- open up,
- be discussed,
- be adopted or adapted beyond its initial context,

then English will become a reference language, at least alongside French.

This transition is not seen as a cosmetic translation,  
but as a **maturity step of the project**.

---

## Long-term vision: I18N

From the outset, the vision includes:
- the coexistence of multiple languages,
- the possibility of translations,
- explicit attention to **internationalization (I18N)** for all structuring content.

French is therefore a **starting point**, not a boundary.

---

## Clear position

> Start in French to think accurately.  
> Move to English to share broadly.  
> Design the project so it does not depend on a single language.

At this stage of the project, **I am structuring the work in French**.
