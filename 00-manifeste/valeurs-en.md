# Core Values and Their Application to the Project

This document driven by commercial interests or fear.This document describes the values that guide my professional approach and explains how they are concretely applied within this data alignment and governance project.

This approach ensures that knowledge remains:
- a shared commons,
- auditable,
- durable over time.

## 1. Open Source and Creative Commons: Sovereignty and Sharing

For me, Open Source is not limited to software code; it is a philosophy of radical transparency.  

---

## 2. Agility: The Power of Small Steps

Agility is understood here as a lever for real effectiveness.  
Rather than pursuing a risky, large-scale overhaul, I favor short, iterative cycles.

This approach makes it possible to:
- demonstrate value quickly,
- adjust based on real-world feedback,
- maintain a positive momentum without exhausting teams.

---

## 3. Data Mesh: Federating Without Centralizing

Data Mesh is the technical and organizational backbone that respects the autonomy of silos.  
Instead of enforcing bureaucratic centralization, this approach creates an infrastructure that connects existing sources.

Technology becomes an invisible binding layer, enabling a global view without depriving contributors of their tools, practices, or ownership.

---

## 4. Humans (With Their Flaws): The Safety Net

This is the central value: accepting people as they are, with their mistakes, omissions, and biases.  
The project does not aim to standardize individuals, but to build a system that supports them.

Imperfection is not treated as a failure, but as a data point—mitigated through redundancy, cross-checking, and dialogue between colleagues.

---

## 5. Visibility: The Antidote to Opacity

Visibility is my primary countermeasure against institutional secrecy.  
By exposing data (metadata, risks, decisions), I create a constructive and positive form of pressure.

By confronting information with operational reality and making it visible to all, we encourage self-correction and break the culture of information retention.
